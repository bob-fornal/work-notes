# Implementing a Script

* Part 1: Task + Identity

    > "I want to [write a LinkedIn post] so that [founders share it with their network]. My role is [AI educator]. The audience is [non-technical business owners]."

    Claude now knows what, why, who you are, and who reads it.

* Part 2: Context Files

    > "First, read these files completely before responding: brand-voice. md, my writing style, and rules for audience research. md, what my readers care about past-winners. md, my top 10 posts by engagement."

    Claude reads your rules before writing a single word. If it is about to break one, it stops and tells you.

* Part 3: Reference

    > "Here is a reference of what I want to achieve: [paste a post you admire]

    Here is what makes it work: Always open with a bold claim. Never use more than 12 words per sentence. Always end with a question."

    You reverse-engineer what works. Claude follows the blueprint.

* Part 4: Success Brief

    > "Type: LinkedIn post, 200 words max. Reaction: The reader screenshots it and sends it to a colleague. Does NOT sound like: generic AI, corporate, jargon-heavy. Does sound like: direct, confident, slightly provocative. Success means: 50+ comments and 200+ saves."

    This is the section that changes everything. Most people skip it. Do not skip it.

* Part 5: Pre-Flight Check

    "Before you write anything:
    
    * List the 3 rules from my files that matter most here.
    * What is unclear or missing? Ask me up to 5 questions.
    * What is the biggest risk of this going wrong?"

* Part 6: Execution Plan

    > "After I answer your questions, give me your plan (5 steps max). For each step, tell me what and why. Only begin when I say Go."

    You see the plan before any writing starts. Bad plan? Fix it before wasting a draft.

* Part 7: Self-Assessment

    > "When you deliver the draft: Mark sections you are most confident about with [STRONG]. Mark sections you are least confident about with [WEAK]. Tell me what you would change with one more revision."

    Claude grades its own work. You know exactly where to focus your review.
